Pride of Sichuan: Master Artisan Receives National Recognition

How Zhu Mulan, provincial representative inheritor of Xinfan Palm Weaving, transformed waste grass fruit stalks into treasures and led communities out of poverty

AW
Editorial Team Aeterna Weave Heritage Stories
Master Artisan Zhu Mulan
Provincial Representative Inheritor of Xinfan Palm Weaving

A Historic Moment of National Recognition

On January 4, 2020, Vice Premier Hu Chunhua of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) conducted an inspection visit to the Pihe Nuzu Township resettlement site in Nujiang Prefecture.

Zhu Mulan, founder of grass fruit stalk weaving and provincial representative inheritor of Xinfan Palm Weaving, reported to Vice Premier Hu Chunhua on how she had used palm weaving techniques to drive local residents toward entrepreneurship and increased income. Vice Premier Hu gave high praise to Zhu Mulan for using palm weaving techniques to transform grass fruit stalks from waste into treasure and leading local communities out of poverty. He encouraged Zhu Mulan to continue her efforts, fully explore the potential of grass fruit stalks, refine and perfect the craft, and turn grass fruit stalk weaving into exquisite products, making positive contributions to targeted poverty alleviation work.

From Waste to Treasure: The Birth of Grass Fruit Weaving

"Grass fruit weaving" utilizes the grass fruit stalks left behind after harvest in the Nujiang Grand Canyon as raw materials. These stalks, once considered completely useless waste that even cattle and sheep wouldn't eat in the eyes of local farmers, have now been transformed into woven handicrafts such as hats, bags, vases, and more, which are sold far and wide. The magnificent transformation from grass fruit stalks to "grass fruit weaving" is inseparable from one person's dedication‚ÄîZhu Mulan, national-level representative inheritor of Xinfan Palm Weaving.

Zhu Mulan is a national-level representative inheritor of the National Intangible Cultural Heritage project of Xinfan Palm Weaving, president of the Xinfan Town Palm Weaving Cooperative in Xindu District, Chengdu, and president of the Xinfan Town Women's Palm Weaving Association in Xindu District, Chengdu.

On October 17, 2019, the sixth National Poverty Alleviation Day, at the Nujiang Prefecture Poverty Alleviation Commendation Conference and Advanced Deeds Report Meeting, 67-year-old Zhu Mulan received the honorary titles of "Social Poverty Alleviation Model" and "First Pioneer of China's Nujiang Grass Fruit Weaving".

The Journey Begins: A Thousand Miles for a Thousand Pounds

Zhu Mulan's connection to grass fruit stalk weaving is inseparable from Xiao Zhi, the person in charge of the Lushui branch of Chengdu Hejia Property Management Co., Ltd. In March 2018, during a conversation with friends in Nujiang's poverty alleviation system, Xiao Zhi learned that Nujiang was eager to find a way to increase income for impoverished communities by utilizing the mountains of accumulated waste grass fruit stalks. Later, through a comrade-in-arms in Chengdu, he contacted Zhu Mulan, hoping she could apply palm weaving techniques to weave Nujiang grass fruit stalks. After some communication, Zhu Mulan agreed, and Xiao Zhi drove thousands of miles to deliver 1,000 pounds of grass fruit stalks to her for experimentation.

Overcoming Challenges: Innovation Through Perseverance

However, the process of weaving grass fruit stalks was far from smooth. Unlike bamboo and rattan which have toughness, grass fruit stalks are particularly brittle and prone to breaking. During the fiber splitting process, Zhu Mulan experienced countless failures, but her character of never giving up until all options were exhausted, combined with her simple yet profound affection for woven products deep in her heart, meant she never stopped trying. When palm weaving tools proved unsuitable for grass fruit stalk weaving, she independently developed row needles for fiber splitting, ultimately thinning the material into the raw materials needed for handicrafts such as hats, bags, vases, and fruit plates.

In March 2019, after more than a year of failures, Zhu Mulan finally succeeded in creating a hat woven from 426 grass fruit stalk fibers, thus beginning the journey of Nujiang "Grass Fruit Weaving". Ordinary Nujiang grass fruit stalks, through the skillful hands of Zhu Mulan and other masters, are wound and interlaced between fingers, transforming into handicrafts for people's daily lives and blooming with new vitality.

From establishing a "grass fruit weaving" poverty alleviation workshop in Fugong County that employed more than 30 local residents from relocation sites, to more than 50 residents at the Lushui City relocation settlement receiving "grass fruit weaving" training, Nujiang "grass fruit weaving" gradually entered people's vision.

Empowering Communities: Training the Next Generation

Since late March 2019, whether at Tuoping Village in Pihe Nuzu Township of Fugong County, Zhiziluo relocation settlement, Yikuaibi relocation settlement in Shangpa Town, or Yezi Flower Residence relocation settlement in Shangjiang Town of Lushui City, one could see Zhu Mulan and the masters she brought from the Xinfan Palm Weaving Association providing on-site guidance to villagers in grass fruit stalk weaving. One-month training sessions at each settlement enabled most villagers to master the weaving techniques for hats, slippers, tissue boxes, and bags. Women with good comprehension abilities, such as A Huamei, Si Sanmei, Na Siyan, Qiu Yiniu, and Cai Chunping, grew into training instructors after one month of training. They not only mastered weaving techniques but also learned to distinguish the quality of grass fruit stalks. In addition to providing woven products to the company for compensation like other trainees, they could also receive salaries from the company.

Building a Sustainable Future: From Craft to Enterprise

Over the past year, Zhu Mulan, who should have been enjoying her golden years with grandchildren, has worked tirelessly, frequently traveling alone between Nujiang and Chengdu, determined to perfect Nujiang grass fruit stalk woven products to the utmost in her lifetime. Meanwhile, Yunnan Jinyun Ethnic Handicraft Culture Inheritance Co., Ltd., jointly established by Zhu Mulan, Xiao Zhi, and Yang Longmei, another Chengdu municipal-level intangible cultural heritage inheritor of Daoming bamboo weaving, has built a bridge of communication between grass fruit weaving craftspeople and merchants. Currently, Zhu Mulan and her team have applied for manufacturing patents for Nujiang "grass fruit weaving." Next, they will rapidly expand product promotion and build the Nujiang brand. At the same time, they are also considering applying for Nujiang "grass fruit weaving" to become a national-level intangible cultural heritage project, so that the skill of "grass fruit weaving" can be passed down from generation to generation.

A Heart for the People: The True Spirit of Craftsmanship

To this day, Zhu Mulan cannot accurately explain what "targeted poverty alleviation" means, but she says that when Xiao Zhi approached her, hoping she would expand Xinfan palm weaving techniques and find a way for Nujiang's impoverished communities to prosper through grass fruit stalks and "earn some money for salt," she agreed without hesitation. She says she recognizes one thing: As long as it benefits the people's development, she is willing to support it, willing to try it, and willing to devote herself wholeheartedly. She believes that Nujiang grass fruit weaving will, like Xinfan palm weaving, bring her a wonderful life and a life of gratitude.

Legacy and Impact: Weaving Hope for Generations

Zhu Mulan's story is more than just a tale of technical innovation‚Äîit's a testament to the power of traditional crafts to transform lives and communities. Her work demonstrates that cultural heritage is not merely something to be preserved in museums, but a living, breathing force that can address contemporary challenges and create sustainable livelihoods.

Through her dedication, hundreds of families in Nujiang Prefecture have found new sources of income. Women who previously had limited economic opportunities now possess marketable skills. Communities that once viewed agricultural waste as a burden now see it as a resource. And a traditional craft that originated in Xinfan has found new life and purpose in the mountains of Nujiang.

The recognition from Vice Premier Hu Chunhua validates not just Zhu Mulan's individual efforts, but a model of development that honors tradition while embracing innovation, that preserves culture while creating economic opportunity, and that empowers communities to build their own futures.

Conclusion: A Model for Cultural Heritage and Development

Zhu Mulan's journey from Xinfan to Nujiang, from palm leaves to grass fruit stalks, from master artisan to poverty alleviation pioneer, offers valuable lessons for anyone working at the intersection of cultural preservation and community development. Her story shows us that:

  • Traditional skills can be adapted to new contexts without losing their essence
  • Cultural heritage can be a powerful tool for economic empowerment
  • Innovation and tradition are not opposites but complementary forces
  • One person's dedication can create ripples of change across entire communities
  • The true measure of craftsmanship lies not just in the beauty of what we create, but in the positive impact we have on others' lives

As we at Aeterna Weave continue to honor and promote Xinfan palm weaving, we draw inspiration from Zhu Mulan's example. Every piece we create, every artisan we support, and every customer who chooses handcrafted palm weaving participates in this same vision‚Äîthat traditional crafts matter, that skilled hands create value, and that cultural heritage can build better futures for all.
